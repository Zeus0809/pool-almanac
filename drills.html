Drills by Levels

Theory is a great thing, but you can never benefit from it without practice. A LOT of practice. This section presents you with a few positional drills that will improve your pattern play and shot making.

Important: General Drill Rules

For every drill in this section, keep in mind the following things as you are doing the drills:

  • You can’t move the cue ball by hand as soon as you’ve made the first ball
  • You can’t touch other balls with the cue ball when getting position for next shots
  • Same rules as in games apply to drills: any regular foul case terminates your attempt

Level 1: Beginner

This set of drills is designed for low level players, those who can make simple balls and are able to do basic cue ball control. You don’t have to put any spin on the cue ball to complete all these drills. Try using natural speed to get position for your next shots.

Level 2: Intermediate

This set of drills is meant for players of intermediate level. If you have strong pool fundamentals, you can consistently make balls and understand how to control your cue ball on the table – these are for you. General drill rules apply.

The 2 by 5 drill

Note

You are expected to make the balls only in the two corner pockets. Attempting to use any other pocket for this drill would be illegitimate.

The Crescent

Note

This drill requires the player to always stay within the crescent area with the cue ball. Crossing the line of balls on any shot is considered a failure.

Level 3: Advanced

Advanced drills are meant for highly skilled players. People who can run a 9-ball or an 8-ball rack are a good fit for this set. Important: Each of these drills is done in numerical order, which means you have to pocket balls in order from 1 or 2 to 9 or 10. Same general drill rules apply.
