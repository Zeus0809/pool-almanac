Francisco Sanchez Ruiz of Spain becomes 2023 world 9-ball champion: how it went

The excitement was palpable as Francisco Sanchez Ruiz faced off against Mohammad Soufi in the pulse-pounding World Pool Championship 2023 final in Kielce, Poland. With a sold-out crowd cheering them on and millions more tuning in on Sky Sports, Matchroom.Live, Viaplay, DAZN, and TV networks around the world, the two pool champions battled it out for the title.

Soufi started strong, taking an early 2-0 lead with his electrifying play, but Sanchez Ruiz wouldn't be deterred. With determination in his eyes, he fought back, clawing his way from behind to take the lead for the first time at 9-8. Soufi tried to keep up, but every time he seemed to gain momentum, another error would give Sanchez Ruiz the advantage. In the end, it was Sanchez Ruiz who emerged victorious, sealing a thrilling victory with a break and run in the 23rd rack. Overcoming the pressure and fighting through every challenge, he claimed his place as the new world champion, adding this title to his US Open Pool Championship, World Cup of Pool, and World Pool Championship titles in the Nineball arena.

With a newfound confidence, the world No.1 now sets his sights on the Premier League Pool and World Pool Masters. "When you win a big one, your confidence goes through the roof," he said. "There's so much pressure in Matchroom events, you cannot compare it to any other." As for Soufi, he remains humble and grateful for the support he received. "I am happy to be number two but I am ready for more," he said. "I feel very good right now. Thank you so much to everyone who has supported me."

It was a match for the ages, filled with nail-biting tension and heart-stopping moments, as two champions brought their all to the table. In the end, Francisco Sanchez Ruiz rose to the top, cementing his place in history as the new World Pool Champion 2023.

"It's an amazing feeling," Sanchez Ruiz exclaimed, struggling to find the words to express his emotions. "I have no voice. I felt the pressure, but I fought for it and I got the end result."

Are you staying in Washington, DC on April 21-23, 2023? Come play against pros in a world ranking event – Bob Stock Memorial 9 ball

First Break Sports Bar in Sterling, Virginia will hold a 9-ball event recognized by Matchroom Multisport as a world ranking event under the Nineball ranking system. That means the venue will see 64 world-class pool players battle it out for $10,000 added! The tournament is open to everybody, no matter age, sex, or skill level. The entry fee is $350 (300 + 50 admin fee), however, there is a number of opportunities to win a free spot in Bob Stocks Memorial through winning one of the qualifier events, namely:

  • Feb 18, Bob Stocks Memorial Qualifier at The Bank Shot Bar And Grill in Laurel, MD
  • Feb 25, Bob Stocks Memorial Qualifier at First Break Sports Bar in Sterling, VA
  • Mar 4, Bob Stocks Memorial Qualifier at On Cue Sports Bar And Grill in Front Royal, VA
  • Mar 18, Bob Stocks Memorial Qualifier at Wolf’s Den Billiards in Roanoke, VA

Plenty of chances to get a free entry and a hotel room covered, because there are more qualifier tournaments to be announced! Follow the tournament director Christopher Lee Wilburn for updates about Bob Stocks Memorial 9-ball.

First ever Ukrainian player claims sponsorship from Predator Cues, the industry leading manufacturer of pool equipment – read more

Vitaliy Patsura is a 26-year-old professional pool player from Ukraine. Being the top-1 player in his country, Vitaliy has won multiple European titles, including Mens’ European 8-ball Championship, U23 and U19 European 9-ball Championship, and placed third at the US Predator Pro Billiard Series Texas Open. Patsura proves to love the game with his whole heart, as he continues to conquer the peaks of American pool in 2023.

A post shared by Vitaliy Patsura (@patsura1)

Only recently, on January 22, Vitaliy Patsura signed an agreement with Predator Cues to join the big guns like Joshua Filler, Fedor Gorst, Eklent Kaci and others in the Pro Team. Huge congratulations, Vitaliy! This achievement must bring Ukrainian pool on a whole new level.
